About Huawei

From Startup to Global Tech Leader

Founded in 1987 by Ren Zhengfei, Huawei began as a small sales agent for phone switches in Shenzhen, China. With initial capital of just ¥21,000 (about $5,000 at the time), the company has grown to become the world's largest telecommunications equipment manufacturer and a leading global provider of smart devices and cloud services.

Today, Huawei operates in over 170 countries and regions, serving more than 3 billion people worldwide. The company invests heavily in research and development, with over 197,000 employees and 114,000 of them working in R&D.

Huawei's mission is to bring digital to every person, home and organization for a fully connected, intelligent world. The company is committed to openness, collaboration, and shared success in the global ecosystem.

Huawei's Interactive Journey

1987-1996

The Founding Era

Huawei was established in Shenzhen with modest beginnings, focusing on manufacturing phone switches and laying the foundation for future innovation.

  • Founded by Ren Zhengfei with ¥21,000 capital
  • Initial focus on PBX switch manufacturing
  • First R&D center established in 1990
  • Shifted from reselling to自主研发 products
1997-2005

Global Expansion

Huawei began its international journey, expanding from China to emerging markets and eventually to developed countries worldwide.

  • First international contract in Russia (1997)
  • Entered European market (2001)
  • Established 12 R&D centers worldwide
  • Annual revenue surpassed $5 billion
2006-2012

Consumer Business Launch

Huawei entered the competitive consumer electronics market, starting with mobile broadband devices and evolving into smartphones.

  • Launched first Android smartphone (2009)
  • Introduced Ascend smartphone series
  • Became top 3 global smartphone vendor
  • Established Huawei Device division
2013-2018

5G Leadership Era

Huawei emerged as a global leader in 5G technology while strengthening its position in premium smartphones and cloud services.

  • Became world's largest telecom equipment maker
  • Partnered with Leica for smartphone cameras
  • Launched P and Mate flagship series
  • Led global 5G standard essential patents
2019-2021

Adaptation & Innovation

Facing challenges, Huawei accelerated innovation in software and ecosystems, launching HarmonyOS and strengthening cloud services.

  • Launched HarmonyOS (2019)
  • Expanded cloud computing services
  • Strengthened R&D in semiconductors
  • Diversified into enterprise solutions
2022-Present

New Frontiers

Huawei continues to push boundaries in AI, cloud computing, and smart ecosystems while maintaining telecom leadership.

  • Expanded HarmonyOS ecosystem
  • Advanced AI and cloud computing services
  • Launched electric vehicle solutions
  • Continued 5G+ innovation
